Welcome to the Cognitive ShuffleiNator!
Use the slider below to pick a session length (10–60 minutes), then tap Start. The app will keep your screen awake, select a random neutral word, speak it aloud, and prompt you to think of words beginning with each letter. Hit Stop at any time to end early. When your time is up (or you press Stop), the session ends, the wake lock is released, and the page will close automatically if your browser allows.

The idea behind a “Cognitive Shuffle”

Dr. Luc P. Beaudoin’s Serial Diverse Imagining (SDI) asks your brain to hop from one simple mental image to the next—a “shuffle” of thoughts that keeps worries and planning at bay and nudges the mind toward sleep. This is the principle behind mySleepButton.

The method:

  • Pick an easy trigger (e.g., a neutral word).
  • Break it into smaller pieces or move on to another unrelated image every few seconds.
  • Because the content is bland and ever‑changing, ruminative loops fade and natural sleep‑onset imagery can take over.
